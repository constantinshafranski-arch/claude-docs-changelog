Automate workflows with hooks

Updated

Clarifies how `ok: false` hook responses behave differently for `Stop`/`SubagentStop` events versus all other hook events.

  • Stop/SubagentStop blocking — When a decision hook returns ok: false on a Stop or SubagentStop event, the reason is fed back to Claude as its next instruction and the turn continues.
  • Other event blocking — For all other hook events, ok: false ends the turn and the reason appears in the chat as a warning line; Claude does not see it.
